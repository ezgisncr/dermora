Moisturizer Selection Guide

Category: Skincare | Published on: April 24, 2025

Moisturizing is one of the most fundamental and critical steps in maintaining skin health. The human skin, as the body's largest organ, is constantly exposed to environmental factors, which can cause damage to the epidermal barrier over time. Moisturizers help support the skin's protective barrier, reduce transepidermal water loss (TEWL), and maintain skin elasticity.

The first step in choosing a moisturizer is to **properly analyze your skin type**. Skin types are generally categorized into five main groups: normal, dry, oily, combination, and sensitive skin. Each skin type has different needs and the ingredients should be selected accordingly:

  • Dry Skin: Choose products containing humectants and emollients like ceramides, hyaluronic acid, and glycerin.
  • Oily Skin: Use lightweight, water-based, non-comedogenic formulas that won't clog pores.
  • Sensitive Skin: Opt for products free of perfumes, alcohol, and harsh chemicals, containing soothing agents like aloe vera and allantoin.
  • Combination Skin: You may need to use different products for different areas (lightweight for the T-zone, richer for the cheeks).

Additionally, it is important to consider the formulation of moisturizers. According to dermatological literature, moisturizers typically contain three main groups of active ingredients:

  1. Humectants: Substances that draw water into the skin (e.g., hyaluronic acid, glycerin).
  2. Emollients: Ingredients that soften and smooth the skin surface (e.g., squalane, shea butter).
  3. Occlusives: Substances that create a barrier on the skin to prevent water loss (e.g., dimethicone, petrolatum).

Today, most moisturizers combine these three groups. Especially during winter, when the humidity is low and water loss increases, moisturizers with more occlusive properties are recommended.

Moreover, using moisturizers containing SPF during your morning routine plays a critical role in preventing early signs of aging caused by UV radiation. Day and night moisturizers are formulated differently: night creams tend to have richer, reparative ingredients, while day creams are lighter and more protective.

In conclusion, selecting the right moisturizer not only provides instant relief to the skin but also helps delay the signs of aging and strengthens the skin barrier in the long term. When choosing a moisturizer, consider your skin type, season, age, and special skin conditions (such as acne-prone skin, atopic dermatitis, etc.).

Remember: A good moisturizer not only softens the surface but also supports the skin’s health from within.
